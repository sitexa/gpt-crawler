*The Great Gatsby*, written by F. Scott Fitzgerald, captures the spirit of the Jazz Age in 1920s America. It tells the tragic story of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire driven by his obsessive love for Daisy Buchanan. Through the eyes of narrator Nick Carraway, the novel explores themes of wealth, desire, illusion, and the decline of the American Dream. With its poetic language and haunting symbolism, *The Great Gatsby* portrays a world of glamour and emptiness, where dreams shimmer brightly but ultimately fade into disappointment.
