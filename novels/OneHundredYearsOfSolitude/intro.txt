Gabriel García Márquez’s One Hundred Years of Solitude, first published in 1967, is a landmark of world literature and a masterpiece of magical realism. The novel tells the multigenerational saga of the Buendía family in the fictional town of Macondo, blending reality with fantasy in a rich, poetic style. Through love, war, politics, and tragedy, the story reflects Latin America’s history and human destiny. Characters live in cycles of solitude, passion, and dreams, blurring the line between myth and history. With its universal themes of memory, fate, and time, the novel remains one of the most influential works of the 20th century.
