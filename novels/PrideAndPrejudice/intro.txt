Jane Austen’s Pride and Prejudice, first published in 1813, is one of the most beloved novels in English literature. Set in rural England, it tells the story of Elizabeth Bennet, a witty and independent young woman, and her evolving relationship with the proud yet honorable Mr. Darcy. Through sharp dialogue and vivid characters, Austen explores themes of love, marriage, class, and personal growth. Elizabeth’s intelligence and Darcy’s transformation highlight the dangers of pride and prejudice in human relationships. With its humor, romance, and social insight, Pride and Prejudice remains a timeless classic admired worldwide.
