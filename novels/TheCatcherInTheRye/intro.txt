J. D. Salinger’s The Catcher in the Rye, published in 1951, is a landmark coming-of-age novel that captures teenage alienation and rebellion. The story is narrated by Holden Caulfield, a sixteen-year-old who has just been expelled from school. Wandering through New York City, he struggles with loneliness, confusion, and his disillusionment with the “phony” adult world. Holden longs to protect innocence, symbolized by his dream of being the “catcher in the rye” who saves children from falling into corruption. With its conversational style and emotional honesty, the novel has resonated with generations of readers as a voice of youthful restlessness.
