Charlotte Brontë’s Jane Eyre, published in 1847, is a classic novel that blends romance, morality, and social criticism. It tells the story of Jane, an orphaned girl who grows up facing cruelty and hardship but develops strength, independence, and integrity. After becoming a governess at Thornfield Hall, she falls in love with her mysterious employer, Mr. Rochester. Their love is tested by secrets, social barriers, and Jane’s determination to stay true to her principles. Celebrated for its emotional depth and strong female voice, Jane Eyre remains a timeless exploration of love, morality, and the struggle for self-respect.
